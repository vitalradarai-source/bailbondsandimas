Step 1 — Arrest and Transport to the Station

San Dimas arrests are handled by Los Angeles County Sheriff's Department (LASD) deputies operating out of the San Dimas Station. Depending on where the arrest happened in the service area — San Dimas, Glendora, La Verne, Covina, Walnut, or Diamond Bar — the deputy may transport the defendant directly to 725 W Arrow Hwy or call for a transport unit.

Transport time is usually 15 to 45 minutes. Once at the station, the defendant is taken to the back booking area, away from the public lobby. Families cannot see or speak with the defendant during transport or initial booking — this is normal, not a sign that something is wrong.

Step 2 — Initial Booking (1 to 3 hours)

Booking is a multi-step intake process. Deputies collect:

  • Personal identification — name, date of birth, address, Social Security number
  • Photographs (mugshot) and fingerprints
  • Medical screening — basic health questions and any medications
  • Property inventory — phone, wallet, keys, jewelry all collected and tagged
  • The official charge from the arresting deputy's report

Fingerprints get run through state and federal databases (CLETS, NCIC) to check for outstanding warrants, prior convictions, parole holds, or probation violations. This database check is the single biggest variable in booking time. A clean record clears in 30-60 minutes. A complicated history with prior cases in other counties can take several hours.

Step 3 — Charge Confirmation and Bail Determination

Once initial booking is complete, the watch commander reviews the charge and assigns the bail amount. There are three ways bail gets set:

  1. Los Angeles County Bail Schedule — most common. Each charge has a pre-set bail amount listed in the county schedule. For example, PC 459 first-degree residential burglary defaults to $50,000. The deputy enters the charge code and the system pulls the schedule amount.
  2. Bail Enhancement — the watch commander or arresting deputy may request bail higher than the schedule if the defendant has prior failures to appear, current probation/parole, or aggravating factors. This requires supervisor approval.
  3. No Bail — for certain charges (capital crimes, parole violations, immigration holds, some domestic violence cases under a CPO), no bail is set at the station. The defendant must wait for arraignment to request bail from a judge.

Important: Bail bonds cannot be posted until the watch commander has officially assigned a bail amount. If the system shows "No Bail" at booking, this often clears at arraignment within 48-72 hours. Calling the bondsman before bail is set is fine — we can monitor and act the moment it posts.

Step 4 — The Bail Bond Process (Once Bail Is Assigned)

This is where families can start moving. Once you confirm the bail amount — either by calling the station directly at (909) 450-2700 or by having the bondsman check the booking system — the bond can be filed.

Step 5 — Release Processing (1 to 4 hours after bond approval)

Even after the bond is approved, there's a final processing step:

  • The defendant is moved from the booking holding area to the release queue
  • Property is retrieved and returned
  • Release paperwork is signed — including the date, time, and location of the first court appearance
  • The defendant is escorted to the front lobby

On a slow Tuesday morning, this entire post-approval phase finishes in about an hour. On a Friday or Saturday night when the station is busy, it can stretch to 3-4 hours.

Why the San Gabriel Valley Booking Window Matters

San Dimas Station is one of the smaller LASD substations, which is good news for families. Smaller stations have shorter booking queues, fewer transfer delays, and more direct access to the watch commander. Compared to bookings that end up at IRC (Inmate Reception Center) in downtown LA — which handles thousands of bookings per day — a San Dimas booking that stays local typically resolves 30-50% faster.

However, certain charges automatically trigger transfer to a larger facility. If your loved one is being held on:

  • Serious felonies (PC §1192.7 list — robbery, kidnapping, sexual offenses, etc.)
  • Murder, attempted murder, or weapons-related felonies
  • Multiple felony charges
  • Out-of-county holds or warrants from other jurisdictions

...they may be transferred to IRC at 450 Bauchet Street, Los Angeles, CA 90012 or to Twin Towers Correctional Facility. Transfers usually happen within 12-24 hours. The bond can be filed at the receiving facility — your bondsman handles the routing.

When Bail Can Be Higher Than the Schedule

Los Angeles County bail schedule amounts are starting points. The watch commander or, later, the arraignment judge can increase bail above the schedule based on:

  • Prior failures to appear — the single biggest factor
  • Current probation or parole status
  • Out-of-state ties or perceived flight risk
  • Aggravating circumstances in the current charge
  • Multiple cases pending across different courts

If schedule bail seems too high to manage, do not assume there is no path forward. Many defendants qualify for an Own Recognizance (OR) release or a reduction at arraignment — your criminal defense attorney files the motion. Until that happens, a bail bond is the fastest route home.

What to Bring When Picking Up Your Loved One

  1. Valid government ID — driver's license, passport, or state ID
  2. Transportation — the lobby is at 725 W Arrow Hwy. Limited public transit. Bring a car or rideshare
  3. A change of clothes if appropriate — the defendant will be released in whatever they were wearing at arrest
  4. Their phone charger — phones are returned but typically dead
  5. Court date paperwork — write down the first court appearance date and location immediately

Bail Bonds in San Dimas: Three Things Families Often Get Wrong

  1. "We need the full bail amount in cash." No. The 10% premium is the only out-of-pocket cost in most cases. For a $30,000 bail, that's $3,000 — not $30,000.
  2. "We have to pay it all upfront." Often no. Payment plans are available for the premium in many situations, especially with a stable cosigner. Discuss options when you call.
  3. "It's like a payday borrowing arrangement." No. A bail bond is a licensed insurance product — a surety bond — regulated by the California Department of Insurance under license category 1800.4. No money is given to you. The bondsman guarantees the court appearance directly.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does booking take at LASD San Dimas Station?

Booking at LASD San Dimas Station typically takes 4 to 12 hours. Misdemeanor bookings on a quiet weekday afternoon can finish in 4 hours. Felony bookings during a busy weekend night may take 10-12 hours before bail can be posted. The single biggest variable is the warrant/wants database check on fingerprints.

Can I post bail at LASD San Dimas Station directly?

Yes. Once booking is complete, bail can be posted at the San Dimas Station front counter or through a licensed bail bondsman by phone. A bondsman files the bond paperwork electronically with the station, the watch commander approves it, then release processing takes another 1 to 4 hours.

What is the address and phone for LASD San Dimas Station?

LASD San Dimas Station is at 725 W Arrow Hwy, San Dimas, CA 91773. Non-emergency phone (909) 450-2700. Serves San Dimas, Glendora, La Verne, Covina, Walnut, and Diamond Bar. Booking and watch commander operations run 24/7.

What if the charge is too serious to hold at the local station?

Some serious felony charges trigger transfer to IRC (Inmate Reception Center) in downtown LA or Twin Towers Correctional Facility. Transfers usually happen within 12-24 hours of booking. Bail can still be posted at the receiving facility — the bondsman handles the routing.

Do I need cash to post bail in San Dimas?

No. With a bail bond, you pay only the 10% statutory premium under California Insurance Code §1800.4. For example, $25,000 bail = $2,500 premium. Payment plans available in many cases. A bail bond is a licensed surety service, not a loan and not financing.
