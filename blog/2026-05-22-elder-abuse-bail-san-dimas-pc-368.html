A defendant in San Dimas is arrested under PC §368 — elder or dependent adult abuse. The statute applies when the victim is 65+ or a dependent adult, and covers physical abuse, financial abuse, neglect, and emotional harm. San Dimas-Specific Facts

PC §368 is California's omnibus elder-abuse statute, covering physical abuse (§368(b)), financial elder abuse (§368(d)/(e)), neglect causing GBI or death (§368(b)(2)), and emotional abuse. Each subsection has different elements and different penalty exposures. The most common SGV case: §368(b)(1) physical or emotional elder abuse — a wobbler with bail typically set at $25,000-$50,000 for felony filings.

San Dimas elder-abuse cases are booked at LASD San Dimas Station and transferred to LA County IRC within 12-24 hours. The transfer adds complications because LASD's elder-abuse intake protocol includes mandatory referral to LA County Adult Protective Services (APS) under Welfare & Institutions Code §15630. APS conducts independent investigation that runs parallel to the criminal case — often producing separate civil findings that can affect criminal plea negotiations.

The protective-order interaction is severe in §368 cases. Under PC §136.2, the court issues a criminal protective order at arraignment that bars contact with the alleged victim. When the defendant is the elder's primary caregiver or shares a residence with the elder (extremely common in SGV multi-generational households), the protective order forces the defendant into alternative housing during the case. This residency disruption is what makes §368 cases so different from other DV-context cases — there's no Family Code §6320 modification path because the victim is presumed legally vulnerable.

Defense strategy in PC §368 cases often focuses on the "abuse" definitional element. The statute requires either willful conduct OR criminal negligence. Mere disagreement, family conflict, or financial disputes between an elderly parent and adult child rarely meet the criminal standard — defense counsel can sometimes negotiate the case down to a civil APS referral with no criminal charges. The bail amount drops proportionally if the DA agrees to reduce from §368 felony to a lesser misdemeanor.

Bail Cost and the 10% Premium

The 10% statutory premium under California Insurance Code §1800.4 is a fee for the surety service, not money lent. Non-refundable regardless of case outcome. No licensed bondsman can lawfully charge a different rate.

Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Don't wait for arraignment — bail can post once booking is complete.
  2. Don't pay cash bail without checking with a bondsman — ties up full amount for 6-18 months.
  3. Don't sign indemnitor paperwork unread — it's a binding contract.
  4. Don't coach the defendant on jail calls — they're recorded.
  5. Don't violate any protective order, even verbally — PC §166 stacks fast.
