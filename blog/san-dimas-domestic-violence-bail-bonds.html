San Dimas Domestic Violence Bail Bonds: How to Get Someone Out Fast

A domestic violence arrest in San Dimas moves quickly. Police respond, an arrest is made, and within hours your family member is booked at the Industry Sheriff's Station — the LA County Sheriff's Department facility that handles all law enforcement calls in San Dimas, a contract city under LASD patrol. Knowing exactly what happens next — and who to call — makes the difference between a night in jail and being home with family.

San Dimas Is an LASD Contract City

San Dimas does not operate its own police department. The city contracts with the Los Angeles County Sheriff's Department, and all arrests — including domestic violence — are handled by LASD deputies. That means booking takes place at the Industry Sheriff's Station, located at 150 N. Hudson Avenue in the City of Industry, roughly 10 minutes from most San Dimas neighborhoods.

This is important for bail purposes. Because LASD processes the arrest, the LA County Bail Schedule applies immediately. There is no separate San Dimas bail schedule — it is the same schedule used countywide. Bail is set from that schedule the moment booking is complete, and a licensed bail agent can begin paperwork as soon as the booking number is assigned.

Key fact: California does not impose a mandatory hold period for domestic violence arrests. Once booking is complete at Industry Station, bail posts immediately from the LA County schedule. Waiting does not help — calling now does.

Domestic Violence Bail Amounts in San Dimas (LA County Schedule)

Bail in domestic violence cases is set according to the LA County Bail Schedule, which assigns amounts based on the specific charge. The two most common DV charges carry the following bail ranges:

ChargeDescriptionTypical Bail
PC § 273.5Corporal injury to spouse/cohabitant (felony)$50,000 – $100,000
PC § 243(e)(1)Battery against a domestic partner (misdemeanor)$10,000 – $20,000
PC § 422Criminal threats (felony — often added)$50,000
PC § 166Violation of protective order (misdemeanor)$5,000 – $10,000

A bail bond costs 10% of the total bail amount — a non-refundable premium set by California law. For a $50,000 bail, the premium is $5,000. For a $100,000 bail, it is $10,000. Angels Bail Bonds works with families on payment arrangements so the full premium does not have to be paid upfront.

Where Your Loved One Will Be Held: Industry Sheriff's Station

Industry Sheriff's Station is the booking and initial holding facility for San Dimas arrests. Most people remain at Industry Station through the booking process. If the jail becomes overcrowded or a longer pre-trial hold is required, transfers to the Men's Central Jail or Twin Towers Correctional Facility in downtown Los Angeles are possible — but Industry Station is the first stop.

Booking at Industry Station typically takes 4 to 8 hours depending on volume. During this window, the defendant is photographed, fingerprinted, and assigned a booking number. Once the booking number exists in the system, Angels Bail Bonds can pull that information and begin processing the bond.

Arraignment at Governor George Deukmejian Courthouse (Pomona)

San Dimas falls within the East District of the LA County Superior Court. Arraignments for felony domestic violence arrests are heard at the Governor George Deukmejian Courthouse in Pomona, located at 400 Civic Center Plaza. For misdemeanor charges, the same courthouse handles initial appearances.

Arraignment typically occurs within 48 hours of arrest on court days. At arraignment, the judge may modify bail — raising it, lowering it, or releasing the defendant on their own recognizance (OR). The East SGV / Pomona Valley area judges are experienced with DV cases and routinely impose criminal protective orders at arraignment, even before conviction.

Protective orders in San Dimas DV cases: Expect the Pomona Courthouse judge to issue an Emergency Protective Order (EPO) at arraignment in nearly every DV case. This order may restrict the defendant from returning home. Angels Bail Bonds can explain what this means for release conditions before paperwork is signed.
